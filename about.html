Founded officially in October of 2018, the society known as Posh Draughts is perhaps best described as a faction in the world of chess; a haven for purists of the game this is most certainly not. Comprising a rag-tag cluster of charlatans, pariahs and pillocks, the Swindon-centric group (which also includes members based in London, Southampton and Karben) has played host to some truly baffling encounters, both over-the-board and by correspondence.

The club is defined by chaos, whether in the woeful organisation of events administered by Desmond 'So Des' Torpey, or the incredible blunders seen so often in the games themselves. However, it is evident that this very disorder is what makes the group such a potent magnet for the provincial chess mavericks it embraces, and hence why its numbers have swelled so drastically (from 4 to 12) in such a short time. This is an environment in which your Darrell Rocks, your Mark Nichollses, your Steve Wellses and your Andy Morses - players otherwise consigned ignominiously to the game's figurative scrapheap - can thrive.

Though, of course, frowned-upon by the chesstablishment, the group offers fans a level of entertainment previously unseen in the chess universe. While Magnus Carlsen may tantalise with the spectacle of almost-godlike tactical nous, such clinical displays cannot compare to the thrill of witnessing yet another queen needlessly relinquished as John Kane fails to notice a lingering bishop.

In-fighting, bad blood and poor sportsmanship are commonplace, but with this all comes unpredictability, calamity, and compelling nonsense. In short: an absolute car crash of a group.
